# Exchange listing policy (fictional example)

> **Fictional document.** This is an invented internal policy for a made-up
> exchange ("Northgate Exchange"), included to demonstrate the scenario of an
> AI agent answering questions from internal company documents. It is not a
> real policy and not advice of any kind.

## Purpose

This policy defines how Northgate Exchange decides which crypto-assets are
admitted to trading, how listed assets are monitored, and when assets are
delisted. It aims to protect customers, meet regulatory obligations and keep
the listing process consistent and auditable.

## Listing committee

Listing decisions are made by the Listing Committee: Head of Compliance
(chair), Head of Legal, Head of Security, Head of Markets. Decisions require
a majority including the chair. Any committee member may veto on regulatory
or security grounds. Minutes are recorded and retained for seven years.

## Admission criteria

An asset must satisfy all of the following before admission:

1. **Legal classification.** External or internal legal review classifying
   the asset under applicable regimes (e.g. MiCA classification for the EU:
   out-of-scope, ART, EMT, or other in-scope crypto-asset) and confirming the
   exchange may offer it in target markets. Assets classified as securities
   in a target market are not admitted.
2. **White paper / disclosure.** Where required, a compliant crypto-asset
   white paper exists and is published; project disclosures are consistent
   with it.
3. **Team and entity checks.** KYB on the issuing entity, screening of
   founders and key contributors against sanctions and adverse media.
4. **Technology and security.** Review of the protocol's consensus and smart
   contract risk; for tokens, at least one reputable third-party audit of the
   token contract; no unresolved critical findings.
5. **Liquidity and market quality.** Evidence of sufficient liquidity on
   reference venues; a designated market-making arrangement for the first 90
   days of trading.
6. **Operational readiness.** Wallet/custody support across our storage
   tiers, tested deposit attribution, and monitoring coverage in our
   blockchain-analytics tooling.

## Ongoing monitoring

Listed assets are re-reviewed at least annually and upon trigger events:
protocol exploits, sanctions designations affecting the project, regulatory
reclassification, sustained liquidity collapse, or governance capture.

## Delisting

The Listing Committee may suspend trading immediately for security incidents
or legal prohibition. Ordinary delisting requires 30 days' customer notice,
withdrawal support for at least 90 days after trading ends, and a public
delisting notice stating the reason category (legal/regulatory, security,
liquidity, project abandonment).

## Conflicts of interest

Employees involved in listing decisions must not trade the candidate asset
from the first committee submission until 14 days after the public listing
announcement. The exchange does not accept payment for listing outcomes;
standard technical integration fees are published.
