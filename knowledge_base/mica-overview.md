# MiCA — Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation: overview

> Informal summary for demo purposes only. Not legal advice. Always consult the
> official text (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114) and qualified counsel.

## What MiCA is

The Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) is the European Union's
harmonised regulatory framework for crypto-assets that are not already covered
by existing financial-services legislation (such as MiFID II financial
instruments). It entered into force in June 2023 and applies in stages:
provisions on asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs) applied
from 30 June 2024, and the remaining provisions — including the regime for
crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) — applied from 30 December 2024, with
transitional arrangements for firms already operating under national regimes.

## Key concepts

- **Crypto-asset**: a digital representation of value or rights that can be
  transferred and stored electronically using distributed ledger technology.
- **ART (asset-referenced token)**: a token that aims to stabilise its value by
  referencing several currencies, commodities, crypto-assets or a basket of
  such assets.
- **EMT (e-money token)**: a token that references the value of a single
  official currency; economically closest to electronic money.
- **CASP (crypto-asset service provider)**: a legal person authorised to
  provide crypto-asset services — custody, operation of a trading platform,
  exchange of crypto-assets for funds or other crypto-assets, execution of
  orders, placing, reception and transmission of orders, advice, and portfolio
  management.

## What CASPs must do

- Obtain authorisation from a national competent authority in an EU member
  state; the authorisation is passportable across the EU.
- Meet prudential requirements (own funds or insurance) scaled to the services
  provided.
- Implement governance arrangements: fit-and-proper management, sound ICT and
  security practices, conflict-of-interest policies, complaint handling.
- Safeguard clients' crypto-assets and funds, including segregation of client
  holdings from the firm's own assets and clear custody policies.
- Publish a crypto-asset white paper for public offers of tokens in scope,
  with prescribed content and no misleading claims.
- Comply with market-abuse rules: prohibitions on insider dealing, unlawful
  disclosure of inside information and market manipulation.

## Stablecoin-specific points

Issuers of ARTs and EMTs face reserve, redemption and disclosure obligations.
"Significant" tokens (by holder count, market value or transaction volume)
fall under enhanced supervision, with a stronger role for the European Banking
Authority. EMT issuance is restricted to credit institutions and e-money
institutions.

## Why this matters for an exchange

An exchange serving EU customers needs CASP authorisation for the services it
offers, must review each listed asset against MiCA classifications (is it an
ART? an EMT? out of scope?), keep white-paper obligations in mind when
admitting tokens to trading, and align custody and client-asset segregation
practices with MiCA requirements.
